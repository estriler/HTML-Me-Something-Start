Candy Store Simulator

Overview

I have always had an interest in video games: how they work, why they are fun, why they can be so addictive. As I've learned to code, I have gained a better understanding of how video games are formulated. This got my creative gears turning and has inspired me to make my own videogame.

The overall plot of the game is to run a successful candy store, possibly the most successful candy store. Every in-game day, you will have your character buy inventory, stock your shelves, open the store, and checkout customers as they arrive, and close up shop before the next day. Rinse and repeat until you have made all the money.

Inspiration

Super Market Simulator


This has been my latest obsession over the last few weeks and overall the biggest inpiration for my Candy Store Simulator idea. I found it very relaxing to buy a box of virtual inventory, have the package delivered to your store, open it up, and place the contents on a shelf. I don't know the psychology behind it, all I know is that it feeds the dopamine machine something fierce and I want to replicate it as best I can in my game.

Stardew Valley


I'm more inspired by the art style and asthetics of Stardew Valley that the actual game mechanics of it. The pixel graphics and pleasing sound effects are going to be a crutial part of my Candy Story Simulator game. I also like the idea of my game having a calander system to break up the repitition each day might bring.

Dave the Diver


Dave the Diver is a big inspiration gameplay-wise. The end of the day when you open up your sushi shop is very much how I see most of the Candy Store Simulator being. Needing the greet customers, do little mini games for them depending on what they want, getting tips depending on how well you do, etc. I also really like how this is done on a 2 demensional field.

Overcooked!

I really like the pacing of each level of Overcooked!, but not so much the stress that comes with it. I picture my Candy Store Simulator game being a lot more chill. Rather than flat out failing a day, you maybe just don't quite make as much money as you would if you did better. I want to reward players for playing well rather than punishing them for playing poorly.

Core Mechanics

  • Setting prices based on current in-game economics
  • Keeping shelves stocked of inventory
  • Keeping backup inventory for when shelves become empty
  • Checking out customers as they approach the counter
  • Weighing out candy based on customer's wants
  • Advertising to promote sales
  • Expanding your stock to sell more variety of products
  • Expanding your store to hold more shelving to sell more products
  • Decorating your store to fit your asthetics and attract more customers and drive up sales

Styling

  • Pastels EVERYWHERE
  • Pixel graphics via Dave the Diver and Stardew Valley
  • Whatever magic Paul Robertson puts into his art

Ideas

  • Seasonal candy selection. Chocolate rabbits, sugar skulls, candy canes, etc.
    • Maybe a calander system ala Stardew Valley??
  • Hiring extra checkers and stockers when your store becomes too big to manage yourself
  • Hiring candy mascots to promote sales and attract customers
  • Self-serve candy machines like gumball machines
