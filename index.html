Candy Store Simulator

Overview

I have always had an interest in video games: how they work, why they are fun, why they can be so addictive. When I started to learn to code, I began to have a better idea on how games work. This got my creative gears turning.

The overall plot of the game is to run a successful candy store.

Inspiration

Super Market Simulator


This has been my latest obsession over the last few weeks and overall the biggest inpiration for my Candy Store Simulator idea. I found it very relaxing to buy a box of virtual inventory, recieve that box in the game, open it up, and place the contents on a shelf. I don't know the psychology behind it, all I know is that it feeds the dopamine machine something fearce.

Stardew Valley


I'm more inspired by the art style and asthetics of Stardew Valley that the actual game mechanics of it. The pixel graphics and pleasing sound effects are going to be a crutial part of my Candy Story Simulator game.

Dave the Diver


Dave the Diver is a big inspiration gameplay-wise. The sushi shop part of the game is very much how I see most of the game being. Needing the greet customers, do little mini games for them depening on what they want, getting tips depending on how well you do, etc.

Core Mechanics

  • Setting prices based on current in-game economics
  • Keeping shelves stocked of inventory
  • Keeping backup inventory for when shelves become empty
  • Checking out customers as they approach the counter
  • Weighing out candy based on customer's wants
  • Advertising to promote sales
  • Expanding your stock to sell more variety of products
  • Expanding your store to hold more shelving to sell more products
  • Decorating your store to fit your asthetics and attract more customers and drive up sales

Styling

  • Pastels EVERYWHERE
  • Pixel graphics via Dave the Diver and Stardew Valley

Ideas

  • Seasonal candy selection. Chocolate rabbits, sugar skulls, candy canes, etc.
  • Hiring candy mascots to promote sales and attract customers
